About Us

Who We Are

Asayake Taiko is a student-led Japanese drumming group at the University of California, San Diego campus. “Asayake” means “morning glow” in Japanese, and invokes the image of the early morning light playing on the ocean mist in La Jolla.

In the spring of 2002, Bobby Koga, Reid Matsuoka, and several other students came together to start a taiko group at the University of California, San Diego. With financial support from the university, the fledgling group purchased various equipment in order to construct the Japanese drums in garages of devoted members. In the spring of 2004, Asayake Taiko at UCSD debuted with their completed drums at the 3rd Annual Nikkei Student Union Culture Show.

Our mission statement is to increase Japanese cultural awareness both within and outside of the UCSD community by sharing our love, passion, and respect for the art of taiko through performances and other events.
